Instruction Decomposition

In a contextual MDP, the policy π(at∣st, ℓ)\pi(a_{t} \mid s_{t},\ \ell)π(at​∣st​, ℓ) is conditioned on a free-form language instruction ℓ∈L\ell \in \mathcal{L}ℓ∈L, where ℓ\ellℓ can be hierarchically decomposed into ordered subtasks c1:K∈LK∼pM(c1:K∣ℓ, s0)c_{1:K} \in \mathcal{L}^{K} \sim p_{\mathcal{M}}(c_{1:K} \mid \ell,\ s_{0})c1:K​∈LK∼pM​(c1:K​∣ℓ, s0​) through VLM M\mathcal{M}M

The full task can be accomplished by solving these subtasks step by step, i.e. conditioning the policy on each subtask π(at∣st, ck)\pi(a_{t} \mid s_{t},\ c_{k})π(at​∣st​, ck​) within corresponding segament uku_{k}uk​, where u1:Ku_{1:K}u1:K​ is an ordered partition of {0, 1, ⋯ , H}\{ 0,\ 1,\ \cdots,\ H \}{0, 1, ⋯, H}

BC Pretraining

Few-Shot Adaptation
