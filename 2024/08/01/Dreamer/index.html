Robust Predictions

It is challenging to reconstruct input and predict reward and returns whose scale can vary across domains. The symplog is used to compress the magnitudes of both positive and negative values

symlog⁡(x)=sign⁡(x)ln⁡(∣x∣+1)symexp⁡(y)=sign⁡(y)(exp⁡(∣y∣)−1)\operatorname{symlog}(x) = \operatorname{sign}(x) \ln (|x| + 1) \quad \operatorname{symexp}(y) = \operatorname{sign}(y) (\exp(|y|) - 1) symlog(x)=sign(x)ln(∣x∣+1)symexp(y)=sign(y)(exp(∣y∣)−1)

The distribution of potentially stochastic targets can be modeled with exponentially spaced bins BBB
